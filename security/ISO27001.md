# ISO/IEC 27001:2022

> ISMS (Information Security Management System) framework + 93 Annex A controls in 4 themes. The EU / global counterpart to SOC 2. Establishes a managed, risk-driven approach to security — not just controls.

---

## 1. ISO 27001 vs SOC 2 — Key Differences

| | ISO 27001 | SOC 2 |
|-|-----------|-------|
| Origin | International (ISO + IEC) | US (AICPA) |
| Deliverable | Certificate | Attestation report |
| Approach | Risk-based ISMS | Criteria-based attestation |
| Structure | Clauses 4–10 (ISMS) + Annex A (controls) | TSCs (Security, Availability, etc.) |
| Audit cycle | Stage 1 + Stage 2 + annual surveillance + 3-year recert | Annual audit |
| Scope statement | Central (Statement of Applicability) | Described in report |
| Customer perception (US) | Strong | Primary |
| Customer perception (EU + global) | Primary | Good |
| Cost | Higher first year | Lower first year |

### Complementary, not competitive
We pursue both. They share ~80% of controls; the other 20% is framework-specific.

## 2. ISO 27001 Structure

```
  Clauses 4–10  —  ISMS requirements ("management system")
     ├─ Clause 4:  Context of the organization
     ├─ Clause 5:  Leadership
     ├─ Clause 6:  Planning (incl. risk assessment)
     ├─ Clause 7:  Support (resources, training, documented info)
     ├─ Clause 8:  Operation
     ├─ Clause 9:  Performance evaluation (monitoring, internal audit, mgmt review)
     └─ Clause 10: Improvement

  Annex A  —  93 controls in 4 themes
     ├─ A.5  Organizational controls (37 controls)
     ├─ A.6  People controls (8 controls)
     ├─ A.7  Physical controls (14 controls)
     └─ A.8  Technological controls (34 controls)
```

2022 revision (replacing 2013) reorganized + rationalized controls. Transition deadline Oct 31 2025 (past — all new certs are 2022).

## 3. Scope — Statement of Applicability (SoA)

**The most important document**. Declares:
- Which Annex A controls are applicable
- Which are excluded (with justification)
- How each applicable control is implemented
- Evidence references

### Our SoA (high-level planning; live version in Drata/Vanta)

Of 93 Annex A controls, we expect:
- **~85 applicable**
- **~8 excluded** with justification

### Likely exclusions for us (a fully-remote SaaS)
- **A.7.4** Physical security monitoring — remote-first; our "office" is WeWork / home; limited applicability
- **A.7.5** Protecting against physical + environmental threats — inherited from AWS
- **A.7.8** Equipment siting + protection — minimal physical infra
- **A.7.9** Security of assets off-premises — applies but limited (laptop policies cover)
- **A.7.11** Supporting utilities — AWS responsibility
- **A.7.12** Cabling security — AWS responsibility
- **A.7.13** Equipment maintenance — AWS responsibility

Note: many of these are "inherited" from AWS rather than "excluded outright"; SoA says so.

### Scope statement (short)
AIMS v2 SaaS platform, operated by <Company>, delivering audit information management services globally. Includes:
- Production infrastructure across supported regions
- All employees, contractors with system access
- Development, staging, production environments
- Third-party subprocessors listed in subprocessor register

Excludes:
- Our customers' environments (they're their own responsibility)
- Marketing website infrastructure beyond where customer data transits

## 4. Clauses 4–10 — The ISMS

### Clause 4: Context
- Understand external + internal issues (competitive landscape, regulatory)
- Identify interested parties (customers, regulators, employees, subprocessors)
- Define scope of ISMS

Artifacts: ISMS scope document, stakeholder analysis.

### Clause 5: Leadership
- Top-management commitment (CEO signed)
- Information security policy (master policy, `implementation/policies/information-security.md`)
- Roles + responsibilities

Artifacts: signed ISMS policy; CISO role description; board minutes discussing security.

### Clause 6: Planning (includes risk assessment)
- Risk + opportunity identification
- Risk assessment methodology
- Risk treatment plan
- Security objectives

Artifacts: risk register; risk assessment report; treatment plan; SoA.

### Clause 7: Support
- Resources (budget, headcount)
- Competence (training)
- Awareness
- Communication (internal, external)
- Documented information (policies + records)

Artifacts: budget, org chart, training records, communications plan.

### Clause 8: Operation
- Operational planning + control
- Risk assessment operationalized
- Risk treatment implemented

Artifacts: operational policies + procedures; ongoing risk management records.

### Clause 9: Performance evaluation
- Monitoring + measurement (metrics)
- Internal audit (annual minimum)
- Management review (annual minimum)

Artifacts: KPIs + dashboards; internal audit report; management review minutes.

### Clause 10: Improvement
- Nonconformity + corrective action
- Continual improvement

Artifacts: CAP register; improvement roadmap.

## 6. Risk-Based Approach

ISO 27001 is risk-driven. Unlike SOC 2 (criteria list), ISO 27001 says: "identify your risks, treat them, monitor them."

### Risk assessment
Per `SECURITY-PROGRAM.md §6`:
- Risk register maintained continuously
- Quarterly deep review
- Annual full re-assessment
- Each risk: likelihood × impact, residual after controls, treatment decision

### Risk treatment
Four options:
1. **Mitigate** (implement control)
2. **Transfer** (insurance, contractual transfer)
3. **Accept** (formally, with sign-off)
4. **Avoid** (stop doing the activity)

Decision documented in risk register with owner + rationale.

### Mapping risks → controls → SoA
Risk identified → control selected (from Annex A or custom) → included in SoA → implemented → effectiveness monitored.

## 7. Certification Process

### Stage 1 — Documentation review
- ~2 weeks
- Certification body reviews ISMS docs
- Gap analysis
- Feedback on readiness for Stage 2

Our readiness signals:
- All policies in place
- Risk assessment + SoA
- Internal audit + management review done
- All Annex A applicable controls implemented

### Stage 2 — Certification audit
- ~1–2 weeks on-site (or remote equivalent)
- Tests controls operating effectiveness
- Interviews staff
- Evidence review
- Sample-based testing

### Outcomes
- **Major nonconformity** — cert delayed until remediated
- **Minor nonconformity** — cert granted with remediation commitment
- **Opportunity for improvement** — non-binding suggestion
- **No findings** — clean

Most first-time passes have 1–3 minor nonconformities. Fixable.

### Certificate
- Valid 3 years
- **Surveillance audits** years 1 + 2 (lighter; ~0.5–1 week)
- **Recertification audit** year 3 (similar to Stage 2)

### Typical year-1 cost
- Stage 1 + Stage 2: $20–40k (certification body)
- Tooling (Drata / Vanta): same as SOC 2 — largely shared
- Internal: 1 FTE for 12 months leading up

## 8. Choosing a Certification Body

Must be accredited (e.g., UKAS, ANAB). Examples:
- BSI
- Schellman
- Prescient
- A-LIGN
- DNV
- TÜV

Criteria:
- Accreditation by reputable body
- Tech industry experience
- Global recognition (if selling globally)
- Reasonable cost
- Responsive

## 9. Internal Audit

Required by Clause 9.2. Annual minimum.

### What
- Independent review of ISMS effectiveness
- Test a sample of controls
- Identify nonconformities
- Report to management

### Who
- Not the same person who implements the controls
- Can be internal (different role) or external consultant
- Phase 2+ we hire external for independence; Phase 4+ maybe a dedicated internal auditor

### Output
- Internal audit report
- Management response to any findings
- Input to annual management review

---

## 10. Management Review

Required by Clause 9.3. Annual minimum (we do quarterly Steering Committee + annual formal).

### Inputs
- Status of prior actions
- Changes in internal/external issues
- Feedback from interested parties
- Risk assessment + treatment status
- Audit results (internal + external)
- Nonconformities + corrective actions
- Monitoring + measurement data (KPIs)
- Fulfillment of information security objectives
- Opportunities for improvement

### Outputs
- Decisions: changes to ISMS, resource needs, improvements
- Minutes kept as evidence

## 13. Gap Assessment

Before formal Stage 1, we perform a gap assessment (internal or consultant):
- List all 93 Annex A controls
- Assess current state (implemented / partial / absent)
- Evidence readiness
- Score vs maturity model

Output: plan with timelines to close gaps before Stage 1.

Consultant cost: $10–25k for a thorough gap assessment. Saves 3-6 months and smoother audits.

## 14. Common Findings in ISO 27001 Audits

- **Risk assessment methodology not consistently applied** — fix: document + training
- **SoA outdated** — fix: review cadence
- **Training records incomplete** — fix: automated tracking
- **Asset inventory drift** — fix: automation
- **Supplier list incomplete** — fix: vendor register rigor
- **Incident records lacking** — fix: IR process discipline
- **Management review minimal** — fix: substantive quarterly reviews
- **Log retention gaps** — fix: immutable archive (we already have)

Most are process hygiene, not technical gaps. Mature programs avoid them.
