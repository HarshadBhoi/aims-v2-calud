# 4.1 — IIA GIAS 2024 Standard Pack

**Parent**: [Phase 4 — Multi-Standard Framework](OVERVIEW.md)
**Estimated effort**: 4-5 days

---

## Objective

Create the IIA Global Internal Audit Standards (GIAS) 2024 standard pack — the second standard pack, proving the abstraction works for a fundamentally different framework than GAGAS.

> **Note**: GIAS 2024 replaced the 2017 International Professional Practices Framework (IPPF) on **January 9, 2025**. The new framework uses a completely different structure: **5 Domains, 15 Principles, 52 Standards** (vs the 2017 IPPF's Attribute Standards 1000-1399 + Performance Standards 2000-2699).

---

## Tasks

### 4.1.1 — GIAS 2024 Metadata & Terminology

- [ ] Standard metadata:
  - Code: `IIA_GIAS`
  - Version: `2024`
  - Name: "Global Internal Audit Standards"
  - Common name: "GIAS"
  - Issuing Body: "The Institute of Internal Auditors (IIA)"
  - Effective date: January 9, 2025
  - Previous version: `IIA_IPPF:2017` (superseded; transition deadline Jan 9, 2025)
  - 120-page document with 4-part structure per standard: Requirements, Considerations for Implementation, Examples of Evidence of Conformance, Topical Requirements (where applicable)

- [ ] Terminology differences from GAGAS:
  - "Engagement" → "Internal Audit Engagement"
  - "Auditee" → "Engagement Client"
  - "Auditor-in-Charge" → "Engagement Supervisor"
  - "Audit Director" → "Chief Audit Executive (CAE)"
  - "QA Review" → "Quality Assurance and Improvement Program (QAIP)"
  - "Finding" → "Observation" (used interchangeably with Finding)
  - "CAP" → "Management Action Plan (MAP)"
  - "Planning Memo" → "Engagement Plan"
  - New: "Risk Acceptance" (when management accepts risk without action)

### 4.1.2 — GIAS 2024 Structure (5 Domains, 15 Principles, 52 Standards)

- [ ] Domain I — **Purpose of Internal Auditing** (standalone purpose statement, no numbered principles)
- [ ] Domain II — **Ethics and Professionalism** (Principles 1-5):
  1. Demonstrate Integrity
  2. Maintain Objectivity
  3. Demonstrate Competency
  4. Exercise Due Professional Care
  5. Maintain Confidentiality
- [ ] Domain III — **Governing the Internal Audit Function** (Principles 6-8):
  6. Authorized by the Board
  7. Positioned Independently
  8. Overseen by the Board
- [ ] Domain IV — **Managing the Internal Audit Function** (Principles 9-12):
  9. Plan Strategically
  10. Manage Resources
  11. Communicate Effectively
  12. Enhance Quality
- [ ] Domain V — **Performing Internal Audit Services** (Principles 13-15):
  13. Plan Engagements Effectively
  14. Conduct Engagement Work
  15. Communicate Engagement Results and Monitor Action Plans

Standard numbering uses **Domain.Principle.Sequence** format (e.g., Standard 9.1, Standard 15.2).

### 4.1.3 — Engagement Types

- [ ] Assurance Engagement (fixed scope, predefined by audit plan; opinion-based)
- [ ] Consulting Engagement (advisory, flexible scope, client-driven)
- [ ] Combined Assurance and Consulting
- [ ] Follow-Up Engagement

---

### 4.1.4 — Finding Elements (5 — differs from GAGAS 4)

- [ ] IIA uses 5 finding elements (recommendation is INLINE, unlike GAGAS):
  ```typescript
  findingElements: [
    { code: "CRITERIA",       label: "Criteria",         isRequired: true, referenceSection: "Standard 15.1" },
    { code: "CONDITION",      label: "Condition",        isRequired: true, referenceSection: "Standard 15.1" },
    { code: "ROOT_CAUSE",     label: "Root Cause",       isRequired: true, referenceSection: "Standard 14.2" },
    { code: "CONSEQUENCE",    label: "Risk / Consequence", isRequired: true, referenceSection: "Standard 15.1" },
    { code: "RECOMMENDATION", label: "Recommendation",   isRequired: true, referenceSection: "Standard 15.1" },
  ]
  ```
- [ ] Finding severity ratings: Critical, Major, Minor, Advisory
- [ ] Classifications allow reclassification after management response (unlike GAGAS)

---

### 4.1.5 — QAIP (Principle 12, Standards 12.1-12.3)

- [ ] **Standard 12.1 Internal Assessment**: Ongoing monitoring + periodic self-assessment (every 3-5 years)
- [ ] **Standard 12.2 External Assessment**: Minimum every 5 years; qualified external assessor (CIA + QA competence); Certified Quality Assessor (CQA) preferred
- [ ] **Standard 12.3 Communication**: QAIP results communicated to senior management and Board
- [ ] Conformance ratings:
  - Generally Conforms (substantial; few improvements)
  - Partially Conforms (deficiencies affecting conformance)
  - Does Not Conform (significant impairment)

---

### 4.1.6 — Independence and Objectivity (Principles 6-8, Principle 2)

- [ ] Individual Objectivity assessment (Principle 2)
- [ ] Organizational Independence (Principle 7): CAE reports functionally to Board
- [ ] 12-month cooling-off period typical (vs GAGAS 24 months — IIA is less strict)
- [ ] Impairment disclosure process
- [ ] Safeguards framework

---

### 4.1.7 — Internal Audit Strategy (NEW — Standard 9.1)

**This is a new explicit requirement in GIAS 2024 (not in 2017 IPPF):**

- [ ] Written Internal Audit Strategy document
- [ ] Aligned with organization's strategic direction
- [ ] Multi-year horizon
- [ ] Updated periodically
- [ ] Approved by Board

---

### 4.1.8 — Report Structure (Principle 15)

- [ ] Report sections (assurance engagements):
  - Title, Background, Objectives, Scope, Methodology
  - Observations (with 5 elements)
  - Conformance Statement (optional — "conforms with GIAS")
  - Management Action Plans
  - Overall Opinion (optional)
  - Distribution List
- [ ] Consulting engagements: lighter format, advisory-focused

---

### 4.1.9 — IIA-Specific Features

- [ ] Audit Charter management (Principle 6, Standard 6.1)
- [ ] Risk-based audit planning (Principle 9)
- [ ] Risk Acceptance Register (when management accepts risk without action — escalate to Board)
- [ ] Three Lines Model coordination (2020 update replaced Three Lines of Defense)
- [ ] Consulting engagement workflow (lighter approval chain than assurance)

## Key Differences from GAGAS

| Aspect | GAGAS | IIA GIAS 2024 |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| Focus | Government accountability | Internal organizational value |
| Scope | US government + federal award recipients | All sectors globally |
| Findings | 4 elements (§6.39) | 5 elements (+ inline recommendation) |
| QA | QA checklist + peer review (every 3 years) | QAIP (internal + external every 5 years) |
| Independence | §3.26 declaration (24-mo cooling off) | Principle 2/7 (12-mo cooling off) |
| CPE | 80 hrs/2yr with 24 govt-specific | 40 hrs/yr for CIA |
| Reports | Formal government report (§6.02) | Management-focused communication |
| Engagement types | Financial / Performance / Attestation | Assurance / Consulting |
| Terminology | Auditor, Auditee | Internal Auditor, Engagement Client |
| Strategy | Planning memo per engagement | Written IA Strategy (Standard 9.1) explicit |
