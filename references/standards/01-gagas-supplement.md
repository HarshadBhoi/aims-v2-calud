## 1. AICPA Integration for Financial Audits

GAGAS financial audits **incorporate AICPA Statements on Auditing Standards (SAS) by reference**. Auditors performing financial audits under GAGAS must comply with **both** AU-C sections AND GAGAS additional requirements.

### Most Commonly Applied AU-C Sections

| AU-C | Title | Relevance |
|------|-------|-----------|
| AU-C 200 | Overall Objectives | Professional skepticism, reasonable assurance |
| AU-C 210 | Terms of Engagement | Engagement letter |
| AU-C 220 | Quality Control at Engagement Level | Paired with GAGAS Ch. 5 |
| AU-C 230 | Audit Documentation | Paired with GAGAS §8.51-8.56 |
| AU-C 240 | Fraud | Paired with GAGAS §8.31-8.33, §6.22 |
| AU-C 250 | Laws & Regulations | Heavily extended by GAGAS §6.23 |
| AU-C 260 | Communication with TCWG | Communication extensions |
| AU-C 265 | Internal Control Deficiencies | Extended by GAGAS §6.41-6.44 |
| AU-C 300-330 | Risk Assessment Suite | Full integration |
| AU-C 500-580 | Evidence | Core evidence standards |
| AU-C 600 | Group Audits (SAS 149 revised) | Paired with GAGAS group guidance |
| AU-C 700-706 | Reporting | Extended by GAGAS §6.17-6.62 |
| AU-C 935 | Compliance Audits | **Critical for Single Audits** |

### "Additional" GAGAS Requirements Beyond AU-C

1. **CPE** (§4.16): 80 hours/2 years, 24 in government — AU-C has NO CPE requirement
2. **Independence** (§3.18-3.108): Conceptual framework + specific prohibited non-audit services stricter than AICPA
3. **Peer review** (§5.60-5.87): Every 3 years with public report
4. **Reporting on Internal Control over Financial Reporting** (§6.40): **Always required** in GAGAS; optional under AU-C alone
5. **Reporting on Compliance with Laws, Regulations, Contracts, Grant Agreements** (§6.41): Required Yellow Book report — no AU-C equivalent
6. **"In accordance with GAGAS" language** in auditor's report
7. **Distribution statement** on report (§6.65)
8. **Views of responsible officials** (§6.57-6.62)
9. **Confidential/sensitive information** treatment

### Standard Product: Yellow Book Report
Separate from the opinion — titled:
> *"Report on Internal Control Over Financial Reporting and on Compliance and Other Matters Based on an Audit of Financial Statements Performed in Accordance with Government Auditing Standards."*

## 2. Single Audit in Detail (Uniform Guidance, 2 CFR Part 200)

### Threshold (Updated 2024)
- Federal expenditures **≥ $750,000** per fiscal year (historic)
- **Raised to $1,000,000** effective for fiscal years beginning on/after **October 1, 2024**, per OMB April 2024 revision
- Triggers Single Audit (multiple programs) or Program-Specific Audit (one program)

### Major Program Determination — Risk-Based Approach (2 CFR 200.518)

**Four-step process**:

**Step 1 — Identify Type A programs** (threshold based on total federal expenditures):
- ≤ $34M expenditures: Type A = ≥ $750K (or 3% whichever greater)
- $34M-$100M: Type A = $750K + graduated formula
- Larger entities: higher thresholds up to $30M+ for very large recipients
- Everything else = Type B

**Step 2 — Identify low-risk Type A programs** (ALL criteria required):
- Audited as major in at least one of two most recent audit periods
- No modified opinion on program
- No material weakness in internal control over compliance
- No known/likely questioned costs > 5% of program expenditures
- Auditor judgment

**Step 3 — Identify high-risk Type B programs**:
- Risk assessment on relatively larger Type B programs
- At least 25% of low-risk Type A dollar value must be tested via Type B coverage

**Step 4 — Percentage-of-coverage rule**:
- **Low-risk auditee**: at least **20%** of total federal expenditures covered
- **Not low-risk**: at least **40%** coverage

### Low-Risk Auditee Criteria (2 CFR 200.520)
Must meet ALL for 2 preceding years:
- Single Audits performed annually and submitted timely
- Unmodified opinion on financial statements AND SEFA
- No GAGAS material weaknesses
- No material noncompliance
- No Type A programs with modified opinion, material weakness, or questioned costs > 5%

### 12 Compliance Requirements (Compliance Supplement, Part 3)

Historically 14, reduced to 12:

| Code | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| **A** | Activities Allowed/Unallowed |
| **B** | Allowable Costs/Cost Principles |
| **C** | Cash Management |
| **E** | Eligibility |
| **F** | Equipment and Real Property Management |
| **G** | Matching, Level of Effort, Earmarking |
| **H** | Period of Performance |
| **I** | Procurement, Suspension, Debarment |
| **J** | Program Income |
| **L** | Reporting |
| **M** | Subrecipient Monitoring |
| **N** | Special Tests and Provisions |

(Deleted/merged: D Davis-Bacon into N; K Real Property merged with F.)

Per program, the Compliance Supplement marks which are **applicable and direct-and-material**.

### SEFA (Schedule of Expenditures of Federal Awards, 2 CFR 200.510(b))

Must include:
- Federal program name + CFDA/ALN (Assistance Listing Number, replaced CFDA)
- Federal agency
- Pass-through entity name + identifying number (if subrecipient)
- Total federal expenditures (cash, accrual, or mixed — disclosed)
- Loans/loan guarantees outstanding balances
- Noncash assistance (food commodities, donated surplus property)
- Amounts passed through to subrecipients
- De minimis indirect cost rate disclosure (if elected)
- Notes on basis of presentation and significant accounting policies

### Data Collection Form (SF-SAC)
- Submitted via **Federal Audit Clearinghouse (FAC)** — migrated from Census Bureau to **GSA** in October 2023
- Submission deadline: **earlier of 30 days after receipt of auditor's report OR 9 months after fiscal year-end**
- Contains: auditee info, auditor info, FS opinion, major programs tested, findings summary, questioned costs

### Cognizant vs Oversight Agency

**Cognizant Agency for Audit** (2 CFR 200.513(a)):
- Assigned when auditee has **≥ $50 million** in direct federal funding in designated base year
- Reassessed every 5 years
- Responsibilities: technical audit advice, quality reviews, resolve cross-cutting issues, coordinate desk reviews

**Oversight Agency for Audit** (default):
- Auditees below $50M threshold
- Federal awarding agency providing the **most direct funding**
- Lighter responsibilities — primarily technical advice

### Questioned Costs (Uniform Guidance)

Thresholds:
- **Known questioned costs > $25,000** per compliance requirement per major program: **must be reported**
- **Likely questioned costs** (projected): reported if material
- Nature, dollar amount, cause, recommendations required

### Findings Elements in Single Audit (2 CFR 200.516(b))

Single Audit findings must include:
- Federal program and ALN/CFDA
- Criteria / condition / cause / effect
- Identification of questioned costs (known + likely)
- Statistical sample info if applicable
- **Repeat finding indicator** (prior year finding number if applicable)
- Recommendations
- Views of responsible officials (when auditee disagrees)

### Single Audit Reporting Package

1. Financial statements + SEFA
2. Auditor's report on FS
3. Yellow Book report (IC + compliance over FS)
4. Single Audit report (IC + compliance over major programs)
5. Schedule of findings and questioned costs
6. Summary Schedule of Prior Audit Findings
7. Corrective Action Plan (prepared by auditee)

## 3. Fraud Considerations (§3.88, §6.22, §8.31-8.33)

### Auditor's Responsibilities

GAGAS incorporates AU-C 240 for fraud risk assessment but adds:

1. **§6.22** (financial) / **§9.31** (performance): Design audit to provide reasonable assurance of detecting fraud **material to audit objectives**
2. **Fraud brainstorming** required at engagement team level
3. **Professional skepticism elevated** — questioning mind + critical assessment of evidence
4. **Presumption of fraud risk in revenue recognition** (from AU-C 240)
5. **Management override of controls** treated as significant risk

### Reporting Fraud (§6.25-6.26)

Auditors must report fraud when:
- Significant within context of audit objectives
- Abuse material to audit objectives

**Reporting chain**:
- Report to **auditee management at level above involved persons**
- If management involved → **those charged with governance** (audit committee, board)
- If governance involved → **external parties** (funding agency, regulator)
- **Direct reporting to external parties required** if:
  - Auditee fails to report as required by law
  - Auditee fails to take timely corrective action
- Include in audit report unless precluded by law or would compromise investigation

### Chain of Custody for Fraud Evidence

GAGAS requirements:
- **Secure evidence** immediately upon identification
- **Document access and handling** — who touched what, when, why
- **Consult with legal counsel and/or investigators** (forensic specialists, OIG)
- **Preserve original documents** — do not alter
- Transition to investigators per established protocols (law enforcement if criminal)
- Document the handoff
- Digital evidence per forensic standards (hash values, imaging, metadata preservation)

**Best practice**: pause audit on affected area once fraud indicated, let investigators take over evidence trail, resume with documented coordination.

## 4. Waste and Abuse

### Definitions (§6.21 financial, §9.32 performance)

**Waste**: Using or expending resources carelessly, extravagantly, or to no purpose. Taxpayers don't receive reasonable value due to inappropriate act or omission by parties having control over government resources. Goes beyond fraud and abuse; most waste does NOT involve legal violations.

**Abuse**: Behavior deficient or improper vs prudent person's reasonable business practice, EXCLUDING fraud and noncompliance. Includes misuse of authority/position for personal financial interests or those of immediate family/close business associates.

### Reporting Differences
- **Fraud/noncompliance/IC deficiencies**: Auditor **must** report if material/significant
- **Waste**: Report when material/significant within audit objectives; may be reported even when quantitatively immaterial if qualitatively significant
- **Abuse**: Not required to report, BUT encouraged when significant. Cautioned against determinations beyond available evidence.

### §6.22 Considerations
- Auditors NOT required to design procedures to detect waste/abuse
- If indicators arise, must extend steps to determine occurrence
- If material, include in report; if not, communicate in management letter

## 5. Compliance with Laws and Regulations (§6.23)

### Direct vs Material Effect Framework

**Direct and material effect on financial statements**:
- Noncompliance directly affects FS amounts/disclosures
- Examples: tax law affecting revenue recognition, Medicaid billing rules affecting A/R valuation
- Auditor designs specific procedures to test compliance

**Indirect effect**:
- Operating/licensing laws not directly affecting FS but potential material consequences (license revocation)
- Auditor performs inquiries + inspections of regulator correspondence
- No detailed procedures unless indications arise

### Testing Compliance

Procedures typically include:
- Understand legal/regulatory framework
- Inquiry of management + legal counsel (legal representation letter)
- Review minutes, regulator correspondence
- Read regulatory reports and examinations
- Inspect licenses, permits, grant agreements
- Specific tests for direct/material provisions
- Specialist involvement (tax, legal) when needed

### Reporting (§6.40-6.42)

GAGAS compliance report states whether noncompliance identified that could have material FS effect. Noncompliance reported by:
- Nature
- Frequency
- Magnitude
- Cause / internal control deficiency that allowed it
- Effect

## 6. IT Considerations (§8.57-8.60)

### When IT Specialist Required
Triggers:
- Complex IT environment with significant reliance on automated controls
- ERP systems (SAP, Oracle, PeopleSoft)
- Custom-developed applications processing significant transactions
- Electronic evidence where form/integrity requires assessment
- Significant IT changes during period (conversions, upgrades)
- Cloud computing arrangements and third-party IT service providers (SOC 1/SOC 2 reports)
- Cybersecurity risk assessment
- Data analytics involving large data extractions

### IT General Controls (ITGCs) — Pervasive Controls
1. **Access to programs and data** (logical access, user provisioning, PAM, SoD)
2. **Program changes** (change management, testing, approval)
3. **Program development** (SDLC — requirements, coding standards, UAT, deployment)
4. **Computer operations** (job scheduling, backup/recovery, incident management, monitoring)

If ITGCs ineffective → **cannot rely on automated application controls**. Must perform substantive procedures OR test compensating manual controls.

### Application Controls
Within a specific application ensuring completeness, accuracy, validity, authorization:
- Input controls (edit checks, format validation)
- Processing controls (calculations, reasonableness checks)
- Output controls (distribution, reconciliations)
- Master file / reference data controls

**Benchmark approach**: Application controls can be tested **once and relied upon across the period** IF ITGCs are effective.

## 7. Audit Documentation (§8.51-8.56)

### "Experienced Auditor" Standard (§8.51-8.52)

Documentation must enable an experienced auditor (with NO previous connection to the audit) to understand:

1. Nature, timing, extent of procedures performed
2. Results and evidence obtained
3. Significant findings, issues, conclusions, judgments made
4. Who performed the work and when (preparer, date)
5. Who reviewed the work and when (reviewer, date)
6. Evidence of supervisory review before report issuance
7. Departures from GAGAS (if any) and rationale
8. Discussions of significant findings with management and TCWG
9. Evidence supporting independence compliance conclusions

**"Experienced auditor"** defined: practical audit experience, reasonable understanding of audit processes, understanding of GAGAS and applicable legal/regulatory requirements, understanding of subject matter and reporting issues, BUT with **no previous connection** with the engagement.

### Retention
- **Minimum 3 years** from issuance of report under 2 CFR 200.334 (Single Audit context)
- **5-7 years** common for state/local practice
- **7 years** to align with statute of limitations
- **Legal hold**: indefinite during litigation/investigation
- Electronic documentation must be protected, accessible, have integrity controls

## 8. Sampling (§8.36)

GAGAS references AU-C 530 (Audit Sampling) and accepts both statistical and non-statistical sampling.

### Statistical vs Non-Statistical

**Statistical**:
- Random selection
- Quantitative measurement of sampling risk
- Methods: Attribute (compliance), Classical variables, Monetary Unit Sampling (MUS/PPS)
- Allows projection of error/deviation to population with measured precision

**Non-Statistical (judgmental)**:
- Auditor judgment in selection
- Cannot quantitatively measure sampling risk
- Must consider all items with "high probability of material misstatement"
- Projection is judgmental

### Sample Size Factors

**Attribute sampling (controls/compliance)**:
- Acceptable risk of overreliance (5-10%)
- Tolerable deviation rate (5-10%)
- Expected population deviation rate
- Population size

**Substantive sampling**:
- Risk of incorrect acceptance
- Tolerable misstatement
- Expected misstatement
- Population size and variability

### Projection of Results
- **Attribute**: deviation rate = # deviations / sample size, compared to tolerable rate
- **MUS**: projected misstatement = taint × sampling interval, + basic precision + incremental allowance
- **Classical variables**: mean-per-unit or difference estimation

### Single Audit Context
Uniform Guidance requires sampling for direct/material compliance requirements for each major program. AICPA Audit Guide provides sample size tables (e.g., 60 items for high assurance at 5% risk, zero expected deviations).

## 9. Performance Audit Specifics (Chapters 9-10)

### Evidence Sufficiency
Judged on:
- **Sufficiency** (quantity)
- **Appropriateness** (quality: relevance + validity + reliability)

Unlike financial audits, **reasonable assurance is NOT tied to materiality** quantitatively — it's tied to audit objectives. Auditor seeks evidence "sufficient and appropriate to provide reasonable basis for findings and conclusions" relative to stated objectives.

Testimonial evidence must be corroborated. Documentary/physical generally more reliable than testimonial.

### §9.60-9.66 Reporting Standards

Performance audit report must include:
- Objectives, scope, methodology
- Findings (structured)
- Conclusions answering audit objectives
- Recommendations
- Reporting on internal control (when significant)
- Views of responsible officials (§9.56-9.59)
- Noncompliance with laws/regulations/contracts/grant agreements
- Fraud, waste (and abuse at auditor's discretion)
- Statement of GAGAS compliance (or limitations)

### The "Matter" Concept
A **matter** is a condition observed that may or may not rise to the level of a finding. Matters less significant than findings may be communicated in report or separately via management letter. Auditor exercises judgment based on:
- Significance to audit objectives
- Potential consequences
- User interests

**Not every observed issue becomes a "finding"** with full four elements — minor matters communicated less formally.

### Performance Finding Structure (§9.16-9.29)
Same four elements: Criteria, Condition, Cause, Effect
Plus optionally: recommendation (tied to cause)

§9.28-9.29: When auditor cannot fully develop a finding (cause or effect not determinable), should still communicate what is known. Full causal analysis critical because recommendations without root cause are likely ineffective.

## 10. Attestation Engagements

GAGAS incorporates AICPA SSAE attestation standards and adds requirements.

### Three Levels of Engagement

| Type | Assurance | Procedures | Report |
|------|-----------|------------|--------|
| **Examination** | Reasonable (high) | Extensive; similar to audit | Opinion on subject matter / assertion |
| **Review** | Limited (moderate) | Primarily inquiry + analytical | Conclusion — "not aware of material modifications" (negative assurance) |
| **Agreed-Upon Procedures (AUP)** | No assurance | Only procedures specified by users | Report of findings only |

### Key Differences

- **Examination** opinion: "In our opinion, the [subject matter] is presented in conformity with [criteria] in all material respects."
- **Review** conclusion: "Based on our review, we are not aware of any material modifications..."
- **AUP**: Lists procedures and findings; states no opinion/conclusion expressed

### Common Government Attestation Subjects
- Examinations of compliance with specific laws/regulations
- Examinations of controls (SOC 1/SOC 2-like)
- Examinations of prospective financial information (forecasts)
- Reviews of interim financial information
- AUP on specific balances, vendor billing accuracy, contract compliance, grant expenditures

## 11. Internal Control Deficiency Reporting (§6.41-6.44)

### Three Levels (from AU-C 265 adopted by GAGAS)

| Level | Definition | Reporting |
|-------|------------|-----------|
| **Control Deficiency** | IC doesn't allow normal prevention/detection of misstatement | Management letter (not required externally) |
| **Significant Deficiency** | Less severe than MW, important enough for governance attention | **Must be reported** in writing in Yellow Book report |
| **Material Weakness** | Reasonable possibility material misstatement not prevented/detected timely | **Must be reported** in Yellow Book report AND Schedule of Findings (Single Audit) |

### Government Context vs SOX

| Aspect | GAGAS | SOX 404 |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Framework | COSO 2013 typically; Green Book (GAO) for federal | COSO 2013 |
| Opinion on IC | **Not required** in Yellow Book (reports on, doesn't opine) | Separate opinion required (large accelerated filers) |
| Reporting | In Yellow Book report (no opinion) | Separate auditor attestation / management assertion |
| Scope | ICFR + Compliance | ICFR only |
| **Compliance-related IC** | **Required reporting** | N/A |
| Who requires | GAGAS auditees (govt, NFPs with fed funds) | SEC registrants (accelerated filers) |
| MW consequences | Could jeopardize low-risk auditee status; CAP required | Market/regulatory sanctions, delisting risk |

### Single Audit Layer
**Internal control over compliance** (each major program): separately assessed and reported. Same three levels (control deficiency, significant deficiency, material weakness) BUT over compliance rather than financial reporting.

## 12. Group Audits

### Governing Standards
- **AU-C 600** (SAS 149, revised, effective for audits of FS periods ending on/after December 15, 2023)
- Risk-based approach replaced old "significant component" framework

### Key Concepts
- **Group engagement partner/team** retains responsibility for group audit opinion
- **Component auditors** may assist — but group team must be involved in:
  - Risk assessment at group level
  - Determining scope of work at components
  - Directing, supervising, reviewing component auditor work
- **Reliance on component auditor** requires assessing:
  - Competence and capabilities (independence, ethics, expertise)
  - Professional regulatory environment
  - Reviewing relevant portions of component auditor's work
- Group team may need to perform or reperform procedures at significant components
- **Two-way communication** required

### GAGAS Group Audit Considerations
- All component auditors must meet GAGAS **independence** and **CPE** requirements
- Peer review coverage — component auditors must have current peer reviews
- Group team should evaluate component auditor's peer review report
- Documentation of reliance assessments
- Communication regarding deficiencies at component level rising to group significance

### Multi-Firm Coordination (Common in Government)
- State audits where local government component audited by a CPA firm
- Federal/state grants flowing down with different auditors at different levels
- Consolidated federal reporting where different agency IGs audit pieces

**Coordination practices**:
- Group audit instructions to component auditors
- Component auditor response templates
- Reliance conclusions documented in group workpapers
- Integrated sampling where possible (avoid duplication)

## 13. 2024 Yellow Book Revision — What's New vs 2018

The 2024 Yellow Book was issued **February 2024** by Comptroller General Gene Dodaro (GAO), effective for:
- Financial audits / attestation / reviews: periods beginning on/after **December 15, 2025**
- Performance audits: beginning on/after **December 15, 2025**
- **Early implementation permitted**

### Major Changes

1. **Quality Management (replaces Quality Control)** — Chapter 5 restructured:
   - Aligns with AICPA **SQMS 1 and 2** (Statements on Quality Management Standards, effective Dec 15, 2025)
   - Shift from compliance-based QC to **risk-based quality management**
   - **Eight components of QMS**: governance/leadership, ethics, acceptance/continuance, engagement performance, resources, information/communication, monitoring/remediation, risk assessment process
   - Annual evaluation of QMS effectiveness by firm leadership
   - Documented root-cause analysis of QC deficiencies

2. **Engagement Quality Reviews** (replaces EQCR):
   - New threshold criteria
   - Separate chapter guidance
   - Enhanced documentation

3. **Key Audit Matters / Critical Audit Matters** — consideration discussed (alignment with AICPA reporting changes)

4. **Fraud and Noncompliance** — expanded discussion; clearer auditor responsibilities for **noncompliance with laws and regulations (NOCLAR)**

5. **Independence — Preparing Financial Statements** — further tightening:
   - Even with SKE (skills, knowledge, experience) documented at auditee, preparing FS remains **significant threat**
   - Stronger safeguards required, or don't perform service

6. **CPE** — retained 80/24 but refined definitions of qualifying subjects; more explicit guidance on ethics CPE

7. **Structural Clarifications**:
   - Renumbered sections in places
   - Improved cross-references
   - Clearer "requirements" vs "application guidance" separation (AICPA Clarity Project format)

8. **Technology Considerations** — expanded discussion of data analytics, cybersecurity, cloud computing

9. **Audit Organization's Monitoring** — must be ongoing (not just periodic), with documented root cause analysis and remediation tracking

10. **External Peer Review** — every 3 years retained; enhanced reporting and remediation requirements; language on public posting strengthened

## 14. Hierarchy of Standards for Single Audit

A Single Audit auditor simultaneously follows:

1. **AICPA GAAS (AU-C sections)** — base standards
2. **GAGAS (2024 Yellow Book)** — adds government-specific requirements
3. **Uniform Guidance (2 CFR 200 Subpart F)** — adds federal compliance audit requirements
4. **OMB Compliance Supplement** — annual document specifying program-specific testing
5. **AICPA Audit Guide: Government Auditing Standards and Single Audits** — authoritative implementation guidance

**All four layers must be satisfied.**
