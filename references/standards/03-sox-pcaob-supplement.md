## 1. Recent PCAOB Standards Updates (2019-2024)

### QC 1000 — A Firm's System of Quality Control (NEW)
- **Adopted**: May 13, 2024 (PCAOB Release No. 2024-005)
- **SEC approved**: September 9, 2024
- **Effective**: December 15, 2025 (firm's system operational; first evaluation report due by November 30, 2026)
- **Replaces**: Legacy QC standards (QC Section 10, QC 20, QC 30, QC 40)
- **Structure**: Risk-based, scalable, aligned with IAASB's ISQM 1 but more prescriptive

**8 Components**:
1. Risk assessment process
2. Governance and leadership
3. Ethics and independence
4. Acceptance and continuance
5. Engagement performance
6. Resources (human, technological, intellectual, financial)
7. Information and communication
8. Monitoring and remediation

**Key innovations**:
- Mandatory **External Quality Control Function (EQCF)** for firms auditing 100+ issuers — independent outside oversight
- Annual evaluation of QC system effectiveness reported to PCAOB on Form QC (new form)
- CEO/managing partner certification of QC system
- Root cause analysis required for QC deficiencies
- Scalability: Small firms have reduced requirements; threshold-based tiering

### AS 1000 — General Responsibilities of the Auditor (NEW)
- **Adopted**: May 13, 2024
- **Effective**: December 15, 2024
- **Replaces/consolidates**: AS 1001, AS 1005, AS 1010, AS 1015
- **Key changes**:
  - Reaffirms fundamental obligations (due professional care, professional skepticism, competence)
  - Establishes engagement partner's overall supervision responsibility more explicitly
  - Clarifies due professional care requires exercising "reasonable care and diligence"
  - Note: Originally proposed 14-day documentation completion deadline (down from 45); final effective provisions may have been modified

### AS 1105 — Audit Evidence (Amendments 2024)
- Addresses **technology-assisted analysis** (ADAs — Audit Data Analytics, including 100% population testing)
- Clarifies 100% population testing via analytics can be appropriate but doesn't eliminate need for evaluating data reliability
- Enhanced requirements for evaluating information produced by the company (IPE/IPC)

### AS 2501 — Auditing Accounting Estimates, Including Fair Value Measurements
- **Adopted**: December 20, 2018
- **Effective**: December 15, 2020
- **Replaced**: Old AS 2501, AS 2502 (fair value), AS 2503 (derivatives)
- Single unified standard for all estimates

**Three approaches to auditing estimates**:
- Test company's process
- Develop independent estimate
- Evaluate subsequent events

### AS 2405 — External Confirmations
- Amended 2023, effective fiscal years ending on/after **June 15, 2025**
- Addresses electronic confirmations

### Proposed NOCLAR Standard (2023)
Would expand auditor's responsibility for identifying **Noncompliance with Laws and Regulations**. Significant industry pushback; status uncertain.

## 2. Critical Audit Matters (CAMs) — AS 3101

### Definition (AS 3101.11)
A CAM is any matter that:
1. Was communicated or required to be communicated to audit committee, AND
2. Relates to accounts or disclosures **material** to financial statements, AND
3. Involved **especially challenging, subjective, or complex auditor judgment**

**All three criteria must be met.**

### Identification Factors
- Assessed risk of material misstatement (including significant risks)
- Degree of auditor judgment in procedures or evidence evaluation
- Nature and extent of audit effort required
- Nature/timing of significant unusual transactions
- Degree of management judgment (high estimation uncertainty)
- Nature/extent of audit evidence obtained
- Effect of significant events or transactions

### How Reported
CAM section in auditor's report must:
1. **Identify** the CAM
2. **Describe** the principal considerations that led to determination as CAM
3. **Describe how** the CAM was addressed (procedures performed)
4. **Reference** relevant financial statement accounts or disclosures

Placement: separate section titled "Critical Audit Matters" immediately after opinion section.

### Effective Dates
- Large accelerated filers: fiscal years ending on/after **June 30, 2019**
- All other filers: fiscal years ending on/after **December 15, 2020**
- **Exempt**: EGCs, brokers/dealers, investment companies (other than BDCs), employee benefit plans

### Typical CAM Examples
- Goodwill and intangible asset impairment assessments
- Revenue recognition for complex contracts (ASC 606 judgments)
- Loan loss allowances / CECL (for banks)
- Income tax uncertainties (ASC 740)
- Business combination valuations
- Insurance reserves / actuarial estimates
- Inventory valuation for complex manufacturing
- Cryptocurrency / digital asset valuation

### CAMs vs KAMs (ISA 701)

| Dimension | CAMs (PCAOB) | KAMs (ISA) |
|-----------|--------------|------------|
| Standard | AS 3101 | ISA 701 |
| Scope | U.S. public issuers | International (non-SEC) |
| Materiality threshold | Must be material | Not explicitly required |
| Criteria | 3-part test incl. "especially challenging" | "Most significant" matters |
| Description of procedures | Required | Required |
| EGC exemption | Yes | N/A |
| "None reported" allowed | Yes (rare) | Yes |

## 3. PCAOB Inspections

### Legal Basis
SOX Section 104. PCAOB Rules 4000-series govern the process.

### Inspection Cycles (SOX §104(b))

| Firm Type | Issuer Audit Clients | Cycle |
|-----------|---------------------|-------|
| Annually inspected | >100 issuers | Every year |
| Triennially inspected | 1-100 issuers | Every 3 years |
| Not inspected | 0 issuers | N/A |

The ~10 largest U.S. firms (Big 4 + BDO, Grant Thornton, RSM, Crowe) are annually inspected. Several hundred smaller firms are on triennial cycle.

### Inspection Process
1. **Selection**: Risk-based sample + random component. Big 4 firm inspection covers 50-60 of thousands of engagements
2. **Fieldwork**: Review workpapers at firm offices, interview engagement teams
3. **Focus areas** (published annually in Staff Inspection Briefs):
   - Recent standards implementation
   - High-risk areas (revenue, estimates, ICFR)
   - Recurring deficiency topics
   - Emerging risks (crypto, cyber, SPACs historically)
4. **Comment forms** issued to firm per engagement
5. **Firm response period**
6. **Report drafting** — PCAOB drafts inspection report

### Part I vs Part II Findings

**Part I (Public)**: Audit performance deficiencies — specific engagement failures
- **Part I.A**: Deficiencies so significant firm didn't support its opinion
- **Part I.B**: Other instances of noncompliance with PCAOB standards (added ~2020)

**Part II (Initially Non-Public)**: Quality control criticisms — systemic firm-wide issues
- Remains confidential for **12 months** after report issuance
- Becomes **public if firm fails to remediate** within 12 months
- "Carrot and stick" incentivizes remediation

### Report Publication
- Part I findings always public
- Published on pcaobus.org
- Firm name, engagement issues (issuer names typically redacted as "Issuer A," "Issuer B")
- Firm's response published alongside

### Firm Remediation (PCAOB Rule 4009)
- Firm submits remediation evidence within 12 months
- PCAOB Board evaluates sufficiency
- If inadequate → Part II becomes public
- If adequate → Part II remains confidential

### Typical Part I Deficiency Rates
- Big 4: historically 20-50% of inspected engagements had at least one Part I deficiency
- Smaller firms: often higher
- 2022 inspection cycle showed uptick — ICFR, revenue, and estimates as top areas

## 4. SEC Filer Categories — Critical for SOX Applicability

### Large Accelerated Filer (Rule 12b-2)
- **Public float**: ≥ **$700 million** (measured at last business day of 2nd fiscal quarter)
- Subject to Exchange Act reporting requirements for ≥ 12 months
- Filed at least one annual report
- 10-K filing deadline: **60 days** after fiscal year-end
- Subject to full SOX 404(a) **and** 404(b)

### Accelerated Filer (Rule 12b-2)
- **Public float**: ≥ $75M but < $700M
- Additional criteria (post-2020 amendments):
  - Annual revenue ≥ $100M **OR** public float ≥ $250M
  - 2020 amendments carved out small-revenue companies from accelerated status
- 10-K deadline: **75 days** after fiscal year-end
- Subject to SOX 404(a) and 404(b)

### Non-Accelerated Filer
- Doesn't meet accelerated criteria
- Public float < $75M, or recently IPO'd, or low revenue
- 10-K deadline: **90 days** after fiscal year-end
- Subject to SOX 404(a) only — **NOT subject to 404(b)**

### Smaller Reporting Company (SRC)
Two-part test (amended 2018):
- Public float < $250M, **OR**
- Annual revenue < $100M **AND** (public float < $700M or no public float)
- Scaled disclosure relief
- **SRC status and Accelerated Filer status are INDEPENDENT** — can be both, or SRC and Non-Accelerated

### Emerging Growth Company (EGC) — JOBS Act 2012
- Total annual gross revenues < **$1.235 billion** (inflation-adjusted)
- EGC status lasts up to **5 years** after IPO, or until:
  - Revenues exceed threshold
  - Becomes large accelerated filer ($700M float)
  - Issues >$1B non-convertible debt in rolling 3-year period
- **404(b) exemption**: Exempt from auditor attestation for duration of EGC status
- Reduced disclosure (2 years of financials, no CD&A, no say-on-pay)
- **CAMs not required** for EGCs

### Foreign Private Issuer (FPI) — Rule 405
- Non-U.S. entity where ≤ 50% of voting securities held by U.S. residents
- Files 20-F (annually) instead of 10-K — due **4 months** after fiscal year-end
- Files 6-K for interim reports
- Can use IFRS without GAAP reconciliation
- Subject to SOX 404(a) and 404(b) if accelerated
- CAMs required (if not EGC)

### 404(a) vs 404(b) Applicability Summary

| Filer Type | 404(a) Required? | 404(b) Required? |
|-----------|-----------------|------------------|
| Large Accelerated | Yes | Yes |
| Accelerated (non-EGC) | Yes | Yes |
| Non-Accelerated | Yes | **No** |
| SRC (if also non-accelerated) | Yes | **No** |
| SRC (if accelerated) | Yes | Yes |
| EGC | Yes | **No** (during EGC period) |
| FPI (accelerated) | Yes | Yes |

## 5. SOX 404(a) vs 404(b)

### Section 404(a) — Management's Assessment
- **Who**: ALL public company issuers (no exemptions)
- **What**: Management must:
  - State responsibility for maintaining adequate ICFR
  - Assess ICFR effectiveness as of fiscal year-end
  - Use a recognized control framework (**COSO 2013 dominant**)
  - Disclose any material weaknesses
  - Cannot conclude ICFR effective if MW exists
- **Where**: Item 9A of 10-K (Item 15 of 20-F for FPIs)
- **Scope**: Entity-level + process-level controls

### Section 404(b) — Auditor's Attestation
- **Who**: Large accelerated + accelerated filers only
- **What**: External auditor performs **integrated audit** providing opinion on ICFR effectiveness
- **Governing standard**: **AS 2201**
- **Output**: Separate opinion on ICFR (can be combined or separate report), "as of" year-end
- **Opinion types**: Unqualified (effective), Adverse (material weakness exists), Disclaimer (scope limitation)

## 6. Integrated Audit Details (AS 2201)

### Integration Concept
AS 2201.06: "Because both audits are integrated, the auditor should design his or her testing of controls to accomplish the objectives of both audits simultaneously."

### Top-Down Approach (AS 2201.21-.40)
1. Identify entity-level controls
2. Identify significant accounts, disclosures, and relevant assertions
3. Understand likely sources of misstatement
4. Select controls to test (those that adequately address assessed risk)

### Dual-Purpose Testing
Single test procedure achieving both:
- **Control testing** (TOE) — does control operate effectively?
- **Substantive testing** — does transaction itself lack misstatement?

### Benchmarking Strategy (AS 2201.B28-.B33)
For **automated controls** with low risk of change:
- If control tested in prior year and effective
- If IT general controls over application effective
- If no significant changes to application/control
- Auditor can "benchmark" — reduce direct testing for up to **3 years** (re-test at least every 3rd year)
- Not applicable to manual controls
- Common for automated application controls (3-way match, credit limit checks)

### Interim Testing Rollforward Factors (AS 2201.55)
- Specific controls being tested
- Degree to which operating effectiveness evidence obtained
- Length of remaining period
- Possibility of significant changes in ICFR
- Control environment and monitoring controls

## 7. PCAOB Sampling Guidance — AS 2315

### Two Types of Sampling

**Attribute sampling (tests of controls)**:
- Measures rate of deviation
- Sample size factors: expected deviation rate, tolerable deviation rate, risk of overreliance (5-10%)
- Common sample sizes (AICPA guide):
  - High control frequency (daily): 25-60 samples
  - Monthly control: 2-5
  - Quarterly: 2
  - Annually: 1
- Zero tolerance for deviations in most SOX sampling

**Variables sampling (substantive tests of details)**:
- Measures dollar amount of misstatement
- Sample size factors: tolerable misstatement (performance materiality), expected misstatement, assurance factor
- Methods:
  - **MUS (Monetary Unit Sampling) / PPS** — weighted by dollar value, most common for balance tests
  - **Classical variables sampling** — stratified, uses statistics

## 8. Fraud Considerations — Beyond AS 2401

### Management Override of Controls (AS 2401.57-.67)
Because management uniquely positioned to override, **always required** procedures:

1. **Test journal entries and other adjustments** (AS 2401.58-.62):
   - **Always required** — not risk-based
   - Selection criteria:
     - Entries to unrelated/unusual accounts
     - Entries to seldom-used accounts
     - Entries by unexpected individuals
     - Entries at period end or post-closing
     - Round dollar amounts
     - No description or little support
     - Intercompany accounts
     - Affecting revenue, reserves, accruals
   - Data analytics now heavily used (ACL, IDEA, Alteryx) for 100% population analysis

2. **Review accounting estimates for bias** (AS 2401.63-.65):
   - Retrospective review of prior year estimates vs actual outcomes
   - Look for bias pattern

3. **Evaluate business rationale for significant unusual transactions** (AS 2401.66-.67)

### Fraud Risk Presumption
AS 2401.41: **Revenue recognition is presumed a fraud risk** unless rebutted (rarely rebutted in practice).

### Related Party Transactions — AS 2410 (Adopted 2014)
- Identify related parties and relationships
- Evaluate company's identification process
- Specific procedures for **each** significant related party transaction:
  - Read underlying contracts
  - Evaluate business purpose
  - Determine if properly authorized
  - Evaluate FS disclosure
- Communicate to audit committee per AS 1301

### Significant Unusual Transactions (SUTs)
- Transactions outside normal course of business, OR
- Unusual due to timing, size, or nature
- Required procedures: business purpose, evaluate support, evaluate accounting/disclosure
- Examples: end-of-period transactions, bill-and-hold, sales with right of return, side letters, roundtrip transactions

## 9. Materiality Calculation Standards — AS 2105

### Planning Materiality (PM) Benchmarks

| Industry / Measure | Typical Benchmark |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| Commercial (profit-oriented, mature) | 3-10% of pre-tax income; **5% most common** |
| Commercial (volatile income) | 0.5-2% of total revenue; or 1-2% of total assets |
| Financial institutions | 0.5-1% of net interest income; or 1% of pre-tax income |
| Investment funds | 0.5-1% of NAV |
| Non-profit | 0.5-1% of total revenue or expenses |
| Development-stage/startup | 1-5% of total expenses or equity |
| Government | 0.5-2% of total expenditures |

### Performance Materiality (AS 2105.07)
- **Typical range: 50-75% of PM**
- Lower end (50-60%): higher risk, history of misstatements, first-year audit
- Higher end (70-75%): lower risk, strong ICFR, recurring clean engagement

### Clearly Trivial Threshold (CTT)
- **Typical: 3-5% of PM**
- Misstatements ≥ CTT tracked on Summary of Misstatements
- Evaluated individually and in aggregate at year-end

### Qualitative Materiality (SEC SAB 99)
Small misstatements may be material if they:
- Mask change in earnings trend
- Hide failure to meet analyst expectations
- Change loss to income (or vice versa)
- Affect compliance with loan covenants / regulatory requirements
- Affect management compensation (bonus thresholds)
- Involve concealment of unlawful transaction
- Relate to segment significant to operations

### Materiality for ICFR Audit (AS 2201.20)
- **Same planning and performance materiality** as FS audit
- Threshold for evaluating control deficiencies differs:
  - Material weakness: "reasonable possibility" of material misstatement
  - Significant deficiency: "less severe than MW, important enough to merit attention"
- No separate "ICFR materiality" — it's about whether control failure could lead to material FS misstatement

## 10. Going Concern — AS 2415

### Auditor's Responsibility
Evaluate whether **substantial doubt** exists about entity's ability to continue for reasonable period — typically **one year from date FS are issued** (aligned with ASC 205-40 effective 2016).

### Process
1. **Identify conditions/events** raising substantial doubt:
   - Negative trends (recurring losses, working capital deficiency, negative operating cash flow, adverse ratios)
   - Loan covenant violations
   - Denial of usual trade credit
   - Need to restructure debt
   - Loss of key customer, franchise, license, patent
   - Labor difficulties, uninsured catastrophe
   - Legal proceedings jeopardizing operations

2. **Evaluate management's plans** to mitigate:
   - Dispose of assets
   - Borrow or restructure debt
   - Reduce/delay expenditures
   - Increase equity

3. **Assess feasibility** of plans

### Reporting Implications
- Substantial doubt + alleviated: No modification; may need disclosure
- Substantial doubt + NOT alleviated: **Explanatory paragraph** — "substantial doubt about ability to continue as a going concern"
- Inadequate disclosure: Consider qualified or adverse opinion
- Scope limitation: Consider disclaimer or qualified

## 11. Subsequent Events — AS 2801

### Type I vs Type II (ASC 855)

| Type | Definition | Treatment |
|------|------------|-----------|
| **Type I (Recognized)** | Conditions existed at balance sheet date | **Adjust** FS |
| **Type II (Non-recognized)** | Conditions arose after balance sheet date | **Disclose** (if material), do NOT adjust |

### Type I Examples
- Settlement of litigation for different amount than accrued (suit filed before year-end)
- Customer bankruptcy with pre-existing poor financial condition
- Return of merchandise sold before year-end

### Type II Examples
- Business combination after year-end
- Loss of plant due to fire/flood after year-end
- Issuance of debt or equity after year-end
- Major customer bankruptcy due to post-year-end events

### Dual Dating (AS 2801.10)
- **Option 1 (Extended date)**: Re-date report to later → auditor responsible for ALL subsequent events through new date
- **Option 2 (Dual dating)**: Keep original date, add specific date for subsequent event
  - Example: "February 15, 20X2, except for Note 12, as to which the date is March 5, 20X2"
  - Responsibility limited to that specific note for extended period

## 12. Use of Specialists — AS 1210 (Updated 2018)

### Two Types
1. **Auditor's specialist (external or internal)** — engaged by audit firm (AS 1210)
2. **Company's specialist** — engaged/employed by company; auditor evaluates work as audit evidence (AS 1105)

### When Required
- Valuations (business combinations, goodwill, complex securities, intangibles, real estate)
- Actuarial calculations (pensions, insurance reserves)
- Petroleum reserves, mineral reserves
- Environmental remediation liabilities
- Complex IT systems
- Tax provisions

### Specialist Qualification Assessment
- Professional certification/license
- Reputation and experience
- Knowledge of reporting framework
- Independence (for auditor's specialist)
- Objectivity (for company's specialist)

## 13. Internal Audit Reliance — AS 2605

### Two Ways External Auditor Uses IA
1. **Reliance on work already performed** — evaluate and use prior IA work
2. **Direct assistance** — IA personnel perform procedures under external auditor supervision

### Competence Assessment
- Educational level and professional experience
- Professional certifications (CIA, CPA, CISA)
- Continuing education
- Policies, programs, procedures
- Work assignment practices
- Supervision and review
- Documentation quality

### Objectivity Assessment
- Organizational status (reports to audit committee? CAE to board?)
- Objectivity policies (rotation, restrictions)
- Whether IA reviews own operational work
- IIA Standards alignment

### Cannot Delegate to IA
- Evaluating significant accounting estimates
- Assessing risk of material misstatement
- Overall audit opinion
- Fraud-related procedures requiring auditor judgment

### Reperformance Requirement
For work external auditor relies upon — reperform a sample of IA's tests to verify quality.

## 14. Group Audits — AS 1206

### Concepts
- **Principal auditor** (group auditor): issues audit report on consolidated FS
- **Component auditor**: audits a component (subsidiary, division, business unit)

### Group Auditor Responsibilities
1. Identify significant components (typically >15% of group assets, revenue, or PBT)
2. Understand component auditor (competence, independence, understanding of group reporting)
3. Involvement in component audits (scales with significance)
4. Evaluate component auditor's work
5. Consolidation procedures (elimination entries, foreign currency translation)

### Two Reporting Approaches
1. **Assume responsibility** — single opinion, principal auditor takes responsibility (common for Big 4 networks)
2. **Refer to component auditor** — "Divided responsibility" opinion

## 15. PCAOB Enforcement & Sanctions

### Investigation Process
1. **Referral sources**: Inspections (Part I.A), tips, self-reporting (Form 3), SEC referrals, restatements, fraud cases
2. **Division of Enforcement and Investigations (DEI)**
3. Informal inquiry → Formal order of investigation (Board approved)
4. **Subpoena power** (Rule 5103)
5. **Wells process** — Wells notice + response opportunity
6. Settlement negotiations or contested hearing
7. Board review of hearing officer's decision
8. **SEC appeal** available

### Sanctions (SOX §105(c)(4))
- **Civil money penalties**:
  - Individuals: up to ~$125,000/violation (~$2.5M for intentional/reckless)
  - Firms: up to ~$2.5M/violation (~$25M for intentional/reckless)
- **Censure** (formal public rebuke)
- **Bar** from association with PCAOB-registered firm (temporary or permanent)
- **Suspension/Revocation** of registration
- **Required remedial measures** (training, QC enhancements, independent monitors)
- **Limitation on activities**

### Recent Enforcement Trends (Williams-Era PCAOB 2022-2024)
- Substantial increase in enforcement actions
- Record fines (KPMG, EY, BDO large penalties in 2022-2023)
- More individual accountability (engagement partners, QC partners sanctioned)
- Focus on: exam answer sharing (EY $100M SEC penalty 2022), independence violations, audit quality failures

## 16. SEC Related Rules

### Regulation S-X (17 CFR Part 210)
Form and content of **financial statements** in SEC filings. Key articles:
- **Article 2**: Qualifications and reports of accountants (includes auditor independence — **Rule 2-01**)
- **Article 3**: General instructions for FS
- **Article 5**: Commercial/industrial companies
- **Article 10**: Interim FS
- **Article 11**: Pro forma financial information

### Rule 2-01 (Qualifications of Accountants) — Auditor Independence
**9 prohibited non-audit services** (SOX §201):
1. Bookkeeping
2. Financial information systems design/implementation
3. Appraisal/valuation services
4. Actuarial services
5. Internal audit outsourcing
6. Management functions or HR
7. Broker-dealer/investment advisory
8. Legal services
9. Expert services unrelated to audit

- Audit committee pre-approval required for permissible non-audit services
- **Partner rotation**: Lead and concurring review partners rotate every **5 years** (5-on, 5-off); other partners 7-on, 2-off

### Regulation S-K (17 CFR Part 229)
Non-financial statement disclosures. Key items:
- **Item 101**: Description of business
- **Item 105**: Risk factors
- **Item 303**: MD&A
- **Item 307**: Disclosure controls (SOX §302)
- **Item 308**: ICFR (SOX §404)
- **Item 402**: Executive compensation
- **Item 404**: Related party transactions
- **Item 407**: Corporate governance (audit committee disclosures)
- **Item 601**: Exhibits (CEO/CFO certifications — Exhibits 31 and 32)

### Key Releases
- **SAB 99** (1999): materiality, qualitative factors
- **SAB 108** (2006): quantifying misstatements (iron curtain + rollover)
- **SEC Release 33-8810** (2007): Interpretive Guidance for Management's Report on ICFR
- **SEC Release 34-88365** (2020): Amended accelerated filer definitions

## 17. COSO ERM 2017 — Enterprise Risk Management

### Full Title
"Enterprise Risk Management — Integrating with Strategy and Performance" (2017)
Replaced COSO ERM (2004) — "Enterprise Risk Management — Integrated Framework"

### Distinction from COSO 2013 Internal Control Framework

| Aspect | COSO 2013 (IC) | COSO 2017 (ERM) |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| Focus | Internal control | Enterprise risk management |
| Scope | Operations, reporting, compliance | Strategy, performance, risk across enterprise |
| Primary use | SOX 404 compliance | Strategic risk management |
| Components | 5 | 5 (different) |
| Principles | 17 | **20** |
| Relationship to strategy | Operational | Integrated with strategy-setting |

### 2017 ERM — 5 Components, 20 Principles

**Component 1: Governance and Culture** (5 principles)
1. Exercises board risk oversight
2. Establishes operating structures
3. Defines desired culture
4. Demonstrates commitment to core values
5. Attracts, develops, and retains capable individuals

**Component 2: Strategy and Objective-Setting** (4 principles)
6. Analyzes business context
7. Defines risk appetite
8. Evaluates alternative strategies
9. Formulates business objectives

**Component 3: Performance** (5 principles)
10. Identifies risk
11. Assesses severity of risk
12. Prioritizes risks
13. Implements risk responses
14. Develops portfolio view

**Component 4: Review and Revision** (3 principles)
15. Assesses substantial change
16. Reviews risk and performance
17. Pursues improvement in enterprise risk management

**Component 5: Information, Communication, and Reporting** (3 principles)
18. Leverages information and technology
19. Communicates risk information
20. Reports on risk, culture, and performance

### ERM vs ICFR
- ERM is **broader**; ICFR is subset of operational/reporting risk
- COSO 2013 IC specifically designed for ICFR assertions (focus on reliability of reporting)
- ERM 2017 addresses strategic, operational, compliance, reporting risks
- **Complementary, not substitute**: most large companies use COSO 2013 for SOX 404 AND COSO ERM 2017 for broader ERM

### Risk Appetite vs Risk Tolerance
- **Risk appetite**: Amount of risk, on broad level, entity willing to accept in pursuit of value (strategic-level, board-approved)
- **Risk tolerance**: Acceptable level of variation in performance relative to objectives (operational-level)
