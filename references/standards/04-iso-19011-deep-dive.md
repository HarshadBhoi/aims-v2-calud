# ISO 19011 — Deep Dive Study

## Guidelines for Auditing Management Systems

---

## 1. Foundation

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Full Name** | ISO 19011:2018 — Guidelines for auditing management systems |
| **Issuing Body** | International Organization for Standardization (ISO) + International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC) |
| **Current Version** | 2018 (replaces 2011 edition) |
| **Scope** | Auditing ANY management system (quality, environmental, security, safety, etc.) |
| **Nature** | Guidance standard (not certifiable itself) |

---

## 2. Who Uses ISO 19011

- Internal auditors (first-party audits)
- Supplier/second-party auditors
- Certification auditors (third-party — ISO 9001, 14001, 27001, 45001 etc.)
- Management system implementers
- Training organizations for auditor competence
- Used across: manufacturing, services, healthcare, government, education

---

## 3. Three Types of Audits

| Type | Party | Purpose | Example |
|------|-------|---------|---------|
| **First-Party (Internal)** | Organization audits itself | Verify internal compliance | Company's quality team audits own QMS |
| **Second-Party (Supplier)** | Customer audits supplier | Assess external organizations | OEM audits component supplier |
| **Third-Party (Certification)** | Independent body audits | Verify conformance for certification | BSI audits company for ISO 9001 cert |

## 4. Complete Structure — All Clauses

### Clause 1: Scope
- Applies to all management systems
- Guidance on planning, conducting, reporting, follow-up

### Clause 2: Normative References

### Clause 3: Terms and Definitions
Key terms: Audit, Auditee, Auditor, Audit client, Audit team, Audit criterion, Audit evidence, Audit finding, Audit scope, Audit plan, Nonconformity, Observation

### Clause 4: Seven Principles of Auditing
1. **Integrity** — Ethical, fair, objective conduct
2. **Fair Presentation** — Honest, accurate reporting
3. **Due Professional Care** — Appropriate judgment and diligence
4. **Confidentiality** — Protect information, use responsibly
5. **Independence** — Impartial; don't audit your own work
6. **Evidence-Based Approach** — Conclusions based on verifiable evidence
7. **Risk-Based Approach** — Focus resources on higher-risk areas

### Clause 5: Managing an Audit Programme
- Establish objectives, scope, resource allocation
- Define roles: audit program manager, auditors, team leaders
- Selection criteria for audits (risk-based frequency)
- Resource planning: budgets, schedules, auditor allocation
- Ensure competence, confidentiality, impartiality
- Documentation and record keeping

### Clause 6: Conducting an Audit — Six Phases

#### Phase 1: Audit Initiation
- Receive request/schedule
- Confirm scope, objectives, criteria
- Identify constraints (site access, timing)

#### Phase 2: Audit Preparation
- Review prior reports, management system documentation
- Establish audit plan (schedule, team, methods)
- Develop checklists and procedures
- Communicate plan to auditee (minimum 2 weeks prior)

#### Phase 3: Opening Conference
- Introduce team, confirm scope, schedule, confidentiality
- Establish communication protocols
- Auditee provides overview, introduces key personnel

#### Phase 4: Collecting Evidence
- **Interviews**: Planned and spontaneous at all levels
- **Observation**: Direct observation of processes and controls
- **Document Review**: Procedures, records, work instructions, logs
- **Testing**: Sample transactions, control effectiveness
- **Measurement**: Metrics, KPIs, non-conformance rates

#### Phase 5: Findings & Conclusions
- Evaluate evidence against audit criteria
- Classify findings (see Finding Terminology below)
- Determine root causes
- Assess risk/significance
- Prepare audit report

#### Phase 6: Closing Conference
- Present findings to auditee
- Discuss nonconformities and recommendations
- Establish expectations for corrective actions
- Finalize audit report

### Clause 7: Competence and Evaluation of Auditors

**Personal Attributes**:
- Ethical, fair-minded, detail-oriented, analytical, diplomatic, self-disciplined

**Knowledge & Skills**:
- ISO 19011 principles, management system standards, industry processes, regulations, risk management, statistical sampling, report writing

**Education, Training & Experience**:
- Secondary school minimum, relevant degree preferred
- Lead Auditor course: 40-80 hours formal training
- Practical experience: 40-120 audit hours under supervision
- CPD: 40-80 hours every 3 years
- Reassessment: every 2-3 years

## 5. Finding Terminology

| Finding Type | Definition | Severity | Action Required |
|-------------|------------|----------|-----------------|
| **Conformity** | Evidence that system meets audit criteria | N/A | Document as positive finding |
| **Major Nonconformity** | Failure significantly affecting system effectiveness or regulatory compliance | High | Mandatory CAP (30-90 days) |
| **Minor Nonconformity** | Isolated failure; limited impact on system effectiveness | Medium | CAP required (30-60 days) |
| **Observation** | Issue not rising to nonconformity level, or positive practice noted | Low | Optional; may address in next audit |
| **Opportunity for Improvement (OFI)** | Suggestion for enhanced performance | N/A | Recommendations, not binding |

### Key Difference from GAGAS/IIA
- ISO 19011 focuses on **system conformity and effectiveness** (does the QMS/EMS/ISMS work?)
- GAGAS focuses on **compliance with criteria and risk mitigation**
- ISO allows positive conformities; GAGAS focuses on issues/findings
- ISO uses "Nonconformity"; GAGAS uses "Finding"; IIA uses "Observation"

## 6. Evidence Types & Requirements

| Type | Description | Reliability |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Records & Documentation** | Procedures, logs, inspection reports, training records | Medium-High |
| **Personnel Statements** | Interviews, verbal confirmations | Lower (must corroborate) |
| **Direct Observations** | Process walkthroughs, visual inspection | High |
| **Measurements & Testing** | Sampling, metrics, KPIs, statistical analysis | Medium-High |
| **Third-Party/External** | Customer feedback, regulatory reports, certifications | Medium |

**Evidence Requirements**: Verifiable, documented, relevant, sufficient, objective

## 7. Relationship to Management System Standards

ISO 19011 is the **universal audit guideline** — one audit standard, many system applications:

| Management System Standard | Focus | ISO 19011 Application |
|---------------------------|-------|----------------------|
| **ISO 9001:2015** | Quality Management | Audit process approach, customer satisfaction, risk-based thinking |
| **ISO 14001:2015** | Environmental Management | Audit environmental aspects, legal compliance, pollution prevention |
| **ISO 27001:2022** | Information Security | Audit access control, incident management, cryptography, business continuity |
| **ISO 45001:2018** | Occupational Health & Safety | Audit hazard identification, emergency procedures, worker participation |
| **ISO 50001** | Energy Management | Audit energy metrics, efficiency requirements |
| **ISO 55001** | Asset Management | Audit asset lifecycle, maintenance, risk management |
| **ISO 13485** | Medical Devices QMS | Audit medical device regulations, risk management (ISO 14971) |

### Integrated/Combined Audits
Organizations with multiple management systems (ISO 9001 + ISO 14001 + ISO 27001) use integrated audit programs — one audit team covers all systems simultaneously.

## 8. ISO 19011 vs. GAGAS / IIA / SOX

| Dimension | ISO 19011 | GAGAS | IIA GIAS 2024 | SOX/PCAOB |
|-----------|-----------|-------|----------|-----------|
| **Purpose** | System conformity & effectiveness | Government audit quality | Internal assurance & advice | Financial reporting controls |
| **Auditor role** | System evaluator | Government auditor | Internal auditor | External auditor |
| **Finding terms** | Conformity, Nonconformity (major/minor), Observation, OFI | Finding (4 elements), Questioned Cost | Observation/Finding (CCCE + Recommendation) | Deficiency, SD, MW |
| **Risk focus** | System capability impact | Misstatement, waste, fraud, non-compliance | Organizational risk | Financial statement materiality |
| **Reporting** | Audit report with corrective action expectations | Formal public report per §6.02 | Internal report to Board | Audit opinion (public filing) |
| **Follow-up** | CAP verification, repeat tracking | Follow-up audit 6-12 months | Periodic management review | Remediation before next SOX cycle |
