## 2. ISSAI Framework Structure

### Founding Principles (ISSAI 1-99)
- **Lima Declaration (1977)**: Basic principles of government auditing — independence, mandate, scope
- **Mexico Declaration (1992)**: Eight principles of SAI independence

### Level 1: Fundamental Principles
- **ISSAI 100**: Fundamental Principles of Public Sector Auditing
  - Independence, transparency, accountability, ethics, quality

### Level 2: Audit Type Principles
- **ISSAI 200**: Financial Audit Principles (cash basis, accrual, combined)
- **ISSAI 300**: Performance Audit Principles (economy, efficiency, effectiveness)
- **ISSAI 400**: Compliance Audit Principles (conformity with law/regulation)

### Level 3: Detailed Guidelines
- **ISSAI 1000-2999**: Financial Audit Guidelines
- **ISSAI 3000-3999**: Performance Audit Guidelines (detailed procedures)
- **ISSAI 4000-4999**: Compliance Audit Guidelines
- **ISSAI 5000-5999**: Other Guidelines (peer review, environmental, IT)

---

## 3. Types of Public Sector Audits

| Type | Focus | Output |
|------|-------|--------|
| **Financial Audit** | Accuracy and legality of financial transactions | Audit opinion on financial statements |
| **Performance Audit** | Economy, efficiency, effectiveness (value-for-money) | Report with findings and recommendations |
| **Compliance Audit** | Adherence to laws, regulations, policies | Compliance opinion/report |

### Performance Audit — The "3 E's"
- **Economy**: Minimizing cost of resources (inputs)
- **Efficiency**: Getting the most from available resources (outputs/inputs ratio)
- **Effectiveness**: Achieving intended outcomes (results vs objectives)

---

## 4. Three Pillars of SAI Independence

| Pillar | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Organizational Independence** | SAI reports to parliament, not executive branch |
| **Functional Independence** | Full autonomy in audit planning, execution, reporting |
| **Financial Independence** | Adequate budgets set by parliament, not subject to executive control |

---

## 5. Key Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Supreme Audit Institution (SAI)** | Constitutional/independent body auditing public sector (e.g., GAO in US, NAO in UK, CAG in India) |
| **Auditee** | Government entity being audited |
| **Audit Mandate** | Legal authority and scope of SAI's audit work |
| **Public Accountability** | SAI's obligation to serve parliament and citizens |
| **Value-for-Money** | Performance audit concept: economy + efficiency + effectiveness |
| **Regularity Audit** | Verifying legality and regularity of transactions |
| **Propriety Audit** | Assessing whether transactions represent prudent use of public funds |

## 6. ISSAI vs. GAGAS

| Aspect | ISSAI | GAGAS |
|--------|-------|-------|
| **Scope** | 190+ countries globally | United States |
| **Authority** | Framework for SAIs; adoption varies by country | Federal requirement for US government audits |
| **Shared roots** | Both derive from same principles of government accountability |
| **Independence** | 3 pillars (organizational, functional, financial) | Organizational + personal independence |
| **CPE** | Varies by SAI | 80 hrs/2-yr cycle (strict) |
| **Audit types** | Financial, Performance, Compliance | Financial, Performance, Attestation |
| **Finding elements** | Similar to GAGAS 4 elements | 4 elements (Criteria, Condition, Cause, Effect) |
| **Quality** | Peer review per ISSAI 5600 | Peer review every 3 years |

## 7. Software Implications

For an AIMS platform supporting ISSAI:
- Same core engine as GAGAS (shared roots)
- Terminology differences (SAI vs audit organization, mandate vs authority)
- Performance audit emphasis on 3 E's (Economy, Efficiency, Effectiveness)
- Multi-language support (190 countries)
- Compliance audit module with legal/regulatory mapping
- Flexible independence framework (3 pillars vs GAGAS approach)
