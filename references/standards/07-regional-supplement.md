## 1. INTOSAI Framework of Professional Pronouncements (IFPP)

### Tier 1: INTOSAI-P (Principles)

| INTOSAI-P | Title | Content |
|-----------|-------|---------|

| **INTOSAI-P 10** | Mexico Declaration (2007) | 8 pillars of SAI independence: (1) Constitutional/statutory framework, (2) Independence of SAI heads, (3) Broad mandate, (4) Unrestricted access to information, (5) Right/obligation to report, (6) Freedom to decide content/timing, (7) Effective follow-up mechanisms, (8) Financial/managerial/administrative autonomy |

## 3. INTOSAI Performance Audit Methodology

### 3 E's Analysis
- **Economy**: Minimizing cost of resources (input costs). Metric: unit cost of input.
- **Efficiency**: Input/output ratio. Technical efficiency (max output from given inputs) vs allocative efficiency (right mix).
- **Effectiveness**: Achievement of intended outcomes/objectives.
- Some frameworks add **Equity** (4th E — fair distribution) and **Ethics**.

### Program Theory / Logic Model
Standard chain:
**Inputs → Activities → Outputs → Outcomes → Impact**
- Inputs: resources (budget, staff)
- Activities: processes/interventions
- Outputs: direct products (services delivered, people trained)
- Outcomes: short/medium-term changes
- Impact: long-term societal effect

### Audit Matrix / Findings Matrix
Structured framework with columns:
- Audit question/sub-question
- Assessment criteria (sources)
- Information required
- Data sources/collection methods
- Data analysis methods
- Expected findings/conclusions

### Performance Measurement Frameworks
- **Balanced Scorecard** (Kaplan & Norton)
- **Results-Based Management (RBM)** — UN standard
- **Theory of Change** — articulates assumptions and causal pathways

1. **Financial Attest Audit**: Verify truth/fairness of accounts. Union (Appropriation Accounts + Finance Accounts). Transition from cash to accrual for ULBs/PRIs.

3. **Performance Audit**: 3E analysis following Performance Auditing Guidelines 2014 (revised to align with ISSAI 3000)
4. **Environmental Audit**: Per WGEA. Examples: Ganga Action Plan, coal block allocation
5. **IT Audit**: IT Audit Manual; audits of GSTN, CBDT systems, PFMS, Aadhaar
6. **Social Audit**: MGNREGA social audits mandated under Schedule I

- **Report on State Accounts** — tabled by Governor in State Legislature
- **Report on Public Sector Undertakings (Commercial)**
- **Report on Revenue Sector** — direct/indirect taxes
- **Compliance Audit Reports** — department-wise (Defence, Railways, P&T, etc.)
- **Performance Audit Reports** — thematic (e.g., 2G spectrum, coalgate, Rafale)

### PAC and COPU
- **PAC (Public Accounts Committee)**: 22 members (15 Lok Sabha + 7 Rajya Sabha), Chair from Opposition by convention. Examines CAG reports on appropriation accounts, finance accounts, departments, autonomous bodies
- **COPU (Committee on Public Undertakings)**: 22 members. Examines CAG reports on PSEs
- **Estimates Committee**: 30 members (all Lok Sabha) — economy/efficiency

### IA&AS (Indian Audit and Accounts Service)

- Structure: Probationer → Deputy AG → Senior DAG → AG/PDA/Director General → Additional Deputy CAG → Deputy CAG → CAG

## 6. Basel IV / Basel 3 Finalisation

### Effective Date
- **January 1, 2023** (revised from delays due to COVID)
- Implementation phased through **January 1, 2028** for output floor

### Key Changes in Basel IV
- **Revised Standardized Approach for Credit Risk (SA-CR)**: Granular risk weights; due diligence requirement even when using external ratings
- **Output Floor**: Internal model RWA cannot be less than **72.5%** of standardized RWA (phased: 50% in 2023 → 72.5% in 2028)
- **SMA for Operational Risk**: Replaces AMA, BIA, TSA. Formula: **ORC = BIC × ILM** where BIC (Business Indicator Component) based on revenue buckets; ILM (Internal Loss Multiplier) reflects historical losses
- **CVA Framework**: BA-CVA (Basic), SA-CVA (Standardized), removed internal model approach
- **FRTB (Market Risk)**: SBA (Sensitivities-Based Approach), DRC (Default Risk Charge), RRAO (Residual Risk Add-On); IMA requires P&L attribution tests

### Basel III Pillars

**Pillar 1 — Minimum Capital Requirements**:
- CET1 ≥ 4.5%, Tier 1 ≥ 6%, Total ≥ 8% of RWA
- Capital Conservation Buffer 2.5%
- Countercyclical Buffer 0-2.5%
- G-SIB surcharge 1-3.5%
- Leverage Ratio ≥ 3% (Tier 1 / total exposure)
- LCR (Liquidity Coverage Ratio) ≥ 100%
- NSFR (Net Stable Funding Ratio) ≥ 100%

**Pillar 2 — Supervisory Review**:
- ICAAP, SREP
- EBA SREP guidelines assess business model, internal governance, capital risk, liquidity risk

**Pillar 3 — Market Discipline**:
- Standardized disclosure templates (DIS31, DIS40, DIS50 etc. under revised framework)

### ICAAP / ILAAP
- **ICAAP**: Annual bank-internal process. Must cover all material risks (Pillar 1 + Pillar 2), stress testing, capital planning over 3-5 year horizon. Documented in "ICAAP Report" submitted to supervisor
- **ILAAP**: Parallel for liquidity — identification, management framework, stress testing, funding plan

### US Stress Tests
- **CCAR**: Fed-led for BHCs ≥ $250B. Qualitative + quantitative. Capital plan required (Form FR Y-14A)
- **DFAST**: Required under Dodd-Frank §165. Annual for largest banks, 2-year cycle for mid-sized. Three scenarios: Baseline, Adverse, Severely Adverse
- **Stress Capital Buffer (SCB)**: Replaced static 2.5% conservation buffer for CCAR banks (2020) — bank-specific based on stress test results

### HITECH Act (2009)
- Part of ARRA 2009
- Extended HIPAA to Business Associates directly (§ 13401)
- **Breach Notification Rule** (§ 13402) — 60-day individual, HHS, media (if > 500)
- Enhanced civil/criminal penalties (up to $1.5M/year per violation category)
- Meaningful Use incentives for EHR adoption
- Accounting of Disclosures expanded
- Restriction request honored when paid out-of-pocket

### OCR Audit Protocol (2016, updated for HITECH)
- 180 audit inquiries organized by Privacy Rule (§ 164.500-534), Security Rule, Breach Notification
- Phase 2 audits focused on BAs + covered entities
- Proposed Phase 3 for 2024-2025

### 2024 Proposed Security Rule Update (HHS NPRM December 2024)
- Removes "addressable" distinction
- Adds mandatory encryption
- Adds mandatory MFA
- Requires vulnerability scans every 6 months
- Requires penetration testing annually
- Adds compliance audits of BAs
- Requires asset inventory

## 9. Other Healthcare Audit Standards

### HITRUST CSF v11 (2023)
- Unified framework mapping HIPAA + HITECH + NIST 800-53 + PCI DSS + ISO 27001 + GDPR + CCPA
- 19 domains, 49 control objectives, 156+ control specifications
- **3 assurance levels**:
  - **e1** (entry, 44 controls, 1-year validity)
  - **i1** (implemented, ~182 controls, 1-year)
  - **r2** (risk-based, 2-year, certification)
- Inheritance model for cloud providers

### CMS Audit Programs
- **RAC (Recovery Audit Contractor)**: Identify overpayments/underpayments. Pay-for-performance contingency fee (9-12.5%). 4 regions
- **MAC (Medicare Administrative Contractor)**: Process claims, local coverage determinations. 12 A/B MACs
- **CERT (Comprehensive Error Rate Testing)**: Measures improper payment rate for Medicare FFS. ~50,000 claims/year
- **UPIC (Unified Program Integrity Contractor)**: Fraud investigations, replaced ZPICs/PSCs
- **SMRC (Supplemental Medical Review Contractor)**: Specific medical review projects
- **TPE (Targeted Probe and Educate)**: MAC-initiated reviews of 20-40 claims for outlier providers

### PHI vs ePHI
- **PHI**: Any individually identifiable health info in any medium — 18 identifiers under § 164.514(b)(2) Safe Harbor
  - Names, geography < state (except first 3 zip digits > 20K pop), dates except year for those > 89, phone/fax/email, SSN, MRN, device IDs, URLs, IPs, biometrics, photos, any other unique identifier
- **ePHI**: PHI transmitted/maintained in electronic form — triggers Security Rule

## 10. ESG / Sustainability — Full Depth

### CSRD + ESRS

**CSRD (Directive 2022/2464)**:
- Transposed into national law by July 2024
- Phased application:
  - FY 2024 (report 2025): large PIEs > 500 employees already under NFRD
  - FY 2025: large companies meeting 2 of 3 (250+ employees, €50M revenue, €25M assets)
  - FY 2026: listed SMEs (opt-out until 2028)
  - FY 2028: non-EU parents with EU activities > €150M
- **EU Omnibus Proposal (Feb 2025)**: Significant scope reduction proposed — raising thresholds, delaying waves 2/3 by 2 years; still under legislative process

### ESRS Set 1 — 12 Standards (Delegated Regulation 2023/2772)

**Cross-cutting**:
- **ESRS 1 General Requirements** — double materiality, time horizons, value chain
- **ESRS 2 General Disclosures** — Basis for preparation, GOV, SBM, IRO, MDR

**Environmental**:
- **E1 Climate Change** — Scope 1/2/3 GHG, transition plan, climate resilience
- **E2 Pollution** — air, water, soil
- **E3 Water and Marine Resources**
- **E4 Biodiversity and Ecosystems**
- **E5 Resource Use and Circular Economy**

**Social**:
- **S1 Own Workforce**
- **S2 Workers in Value Chain**
- **S3 Affected Communities**
- **S4 Consumers and End-users**

**Governance**:
- **G1 Business Conduct** — corruption/bribery, payment practices, political engagement, animal welfare

### Double Materiality
- **Impact materiality** (outward): company's impacts on people, society, environment
- **Financial materiality** (inward): ESG factors impacting company's financial performance
- **Digital tagging**: XBRL required via ESEF

### ISSB Standards

**IFRS S1 (General)**:
- Core content pillars: Governance, Strategy, Risk Management, Metrics & Targets
- Identical structure to TCFD
- Requires disclosure of sustainability risks/opportunities over short/medium/long term
- Industry-based metrics via SASB as "source"

**IFRS S2 (Climate)**:
- Builds on S1 with climate specifics
- Scope 1 (direct), Scope 2 (indirect energy), Scope 3 (value chain, 15 categories per GHG Protocol)
- GHG Protocol Corporate Standard required
- Climate scenario analysis required
- Transition plan disclosure
- Cross-industry metrics: gross emissions, emissions intensity, transition/physical risk exposure
- Transition reliefs: Year 1 optional Scope 3, optional comparatives

**Jurisdictional Adoption**: UK (2025 SDS), Australia (ASRS S1/S2 from Jan 2025), Japan (SSBJ standards 2025), Canada (CSDS 1/2), Brazil, Nigeria, Singapore

### SEC Climate Rule (March 2024)
- Scope 1 & 2 emissions for LAFs and AFs — **stayed by 5th Circuit** April 2024, SEC voluntarily paused March 2025
- Would require: climate risk disclosure in 10-K, material Scope 1/2, attestation (limited then reasonable), governance/risk/strategy, transition activities, targets, financial statement footnote (1% threshold)
- No Scope 3 in final rule

**California equivalents**:
- **SB 253** (emissions — Scope 1/2/3)
- **SB 261** (climate risk disclosure)
- **AB 1305** (offset claims)
- $1B/$500M revenue thresholds

### GRI Standards (2021 Universal + 2016 topic revisions)

**Universal Standards**:
- **GRI 1: Foundation 2021** — 9 reporting principles (accuracy, balance, clarity, comparability, completeness, sustainability context, timeliness, verifiability, stakeholder inclusiveness)
- **GRI 2: General Disclosures 2021** — 31 disclosures across org profile, activities, governance, stakeholder engagement, strategy/policies/practices
- **GRI 3: Material Topics 2021** — 3-step process: (1) understand context, (2) identify impacts, (3) assess significance

**Sector Standards**:
- GRI 11 Oil and Gas, GRI 12 Coal, GRI 13 Agriculture/Aquaculture/Fishing, GRI 14 Mining

**Topic Standards**:
- **Economic (200)**: 201 Economic performance, 202 Market presence, 203 Indirect impacts, 204 Procurement, 205 Anti-corruption, 206 Anti-competitive, 207 Tax
- **Environmental (300)**: 301 Materials, 302 Energy, 303 Water, 304 Biodiversity, 305 Emissions, 306 Waste (2020), 308 Supplier Enviro Assessment
- **Social (400)**: 401 Employment, 403 OH&S (2018), 404 Training, 405 Diversity, 406 Non-discrimination, 407 Freedom of association, 408 Child labor, 409 Forced labor, 413 Local communities, 414 Supplier social assessment, 415 Public policy, 416 Customer health/safety, 417 Marketing/labeling, 418 Customer privacy

### TCFD — 4 Pillars, 11 Recommended Disclosures
- **Governance** (2 disclosures): Board oversight, management role
- **Strategy** (3): Risks/opportunities, impact on business, resilience under scenarios (2°C or lower)
- **Risk Management** (3): Process for identifying, managing, integration
- **Metrics & Targets** (3): Metrics used, Scope 1/2/3, targets
- **Status**: TCFD disbanded 2023, monitoring taken over by IFRS Foundation (ISSB)

### SASB Standards (now under ISSB)
- **77 industry standards** across **11 sectors** (SICS — Sustainable Industry Classification System)
- Each industry has 5 dimensions: Environment, Social Capital, Human Capital, Business Model & Innovation, Leadership & Governance
- Typically 6-13 industry-specific disclosure topics per standard

### Assurance Standards for Sustainability
- **ISAE 3000 (Revised 2013)**: Framework for assurance engagements other than audits/reviews of historical financial info. Reasonable vs limited assurance
- **ISAE 3410**: Assurance engagements on GHG statements
- **ISO 14064-3:2019**: Verification/validation of GHG statements
- **IAASB ISSA 5000**: New proposed General Requirements for Sustainability Assurance Engagements — exposure draft 2024, expected final 2025
- **AA1000 AS v3 (2020)**: AccountAbility assurance standard — 3 principles (Inclusivity, Materiality, Responsiveness, Impact)

## 12. AML / BSA Audit — Deep Dive

### BSA/AML Program Requirements (31 USC 5318(h))
**Five pillars** (originally 4; expanded by AMLA 2020):
1. **Internal controls** — policies, procedures
2. **Independent testing** — annual audit (risk-based frequency)
3. **Designated BSA/AML compliance officer**
4. **Ongoing training**
5. **CDD/Beneficial Ownership** (added May 2018 under FinCEN CDD Rule; AMLA 2020 codified)

### Key BSA Reporting Requirements

| Report | Form | Threshold | Deadline |
|--------|------|-----------|----------|
| **CTR (Currency Transaction Report)** | FinCEN 112 | Cash transactions > $10,000 (aggregated) | 15 days |
| **SAR (Suspicious Activity Report)** | FinCEN 111 | Banks: > $5K identified subject, > $25K regardless; Casinos: > $5K; MSBs: > $2K | 30 days (60 if no suspect) |
| **CMIR** | FinCEN 105 | Cross-border > $10K | At border |
| **FBAR** | FinCEN 114 | Foreign accounts > $10K aggregate | April 15 |
| **Form 8300** | IRS | Cash > $10K from trade/business | 15 days |

### CDD / Beneficial Ownership Rule (2018)
- Identify beneficial owners ≥ 25% equity + one control person

### Corporate Transparency Act (CTA, 2021)
- FinCEN BOI reporting — largely enjoined by federal courts 2024/2025
- Currently limited enforcement

### FFIEC BSA/AML Examination Manual
- Updated 2021, 2022, 2024 — risk-based approach
- Core procedures, expanded procedures for products/services/customers/geography
- **AML Risk Assessment** — customer, product, service, geography, transaction risk
- **Transaction monitoring** — rules, thresholds, automated systems validation
- **Model validation** — OCC Bulletin 2011-12, SR 11-7

### OFAC Sanctions Compliance

**OFAC Framework for Compliance Commitments (2019)**:
5 components:
1. Management Commitment
2. Risk Assessment
3. Internal Controls
4. Testing and Auditing
5. Training

**Key Lists**:
- **SDN List (Specially Designated Nationals)**
- **Sectoral Sanctions Identifications (SSI)**
- **50 Percent Rule** — aggregate ownership

**Country programs**: Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Syria, Russia, Belarus, Crimea/DNR/LNR regions

**OFAC penalties**: $368,136 per violation (2024 CMP adjustments) + per-transaction strict liability

### KYC / CDD Testing
- **CIP (Customer Identification Program) — 31 CFR 1020.220**: name, DOB, address, TIN
- **CDD — 31 CFR 1010.230**: Understand customer nature/purpose, ongoing monitoring
- **EDD (Enhanced Due Diligence)**: High-risk customers — private banking, PEPs (31 CFR 1010.620), correspondent banking (31 CFR 1010.610)
- **PEP screening** — political figures, family, close associates

### AMLA 2020 (Anti-Money Laundering Act — NDAA 2021 Title LXIV)
- Created **BSA whistleblower program** (AMLA § 6314) — up to 30% of sanctions > $1M
- **AML/CFT Priorities** (FinCEN June 2021): corruption, cybercrime, foreign/domestic terrorism, fraud, TCOs, drug trafficking, human trafficking, proliferation financing
- Expanded subpoena authority over foreign banks with US correspondents
- **FinCEN Exchange** information sharing
